Neglect

Neglect isn’t always intentional. Busy parents with multiple children may unintentionally fail to give enough emotional attention. This can lead young people to feel invisible and make it difficult to form deep, trusting relationships later in life.

Bullying

Bullying — especially in middle school — can have long-lasting effects. It often teaches kids to stay silent out of fear. When victims are punished along with bullies, it reinforces the belief that they are always at fault. Over time, this destroys self-worth and confidence.

Abuse

Physical, emotional, or verbal abuse by caregivers is one of the strongest risk factors. When the people you’re supposed to trust the most cause harm, it shatters your sense of safety and often leads to deep trust issues and self-blame.

Other Contributing Factors

Moving away from home, academic pressure, social isolation, and genetic factors can also play important roles.
